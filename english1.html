Kenta Yokota's diary entry for January 19, 2023

Once again, it's a day in the life of a security guard

It's now 9:30pm, and today, as usual, I started my day with a part-time job as a traffic guide. Fortunately, it ended soon. I've been in the security industry for three years now. While it can be tedious and uninteresting to the point of being tedious, in recent times, this industry, if not all industries, is suffering from a shortage of personnel, and as a result, companies are increasingly raising the salaries of their part-time employees. This is a good thing, at least for those in the position of being mere part-time employees. But being a part-timer also means that my employment is unstable, and this is something that is easy for anyone to understand. So why am I not a full-time employee? Is it because I am a dropout, not good enough to be a full-time security guard? I would argue that this is not the case. The reason is that even if I were a full-time employee, the personnel shortage would not change, and additionally, as a 22-year-old born in 2000, the security industry often has many older employees. As such, young people like myself are a valuable presence. I am a high school dropout, but I am a student at The Open University of Japan (OUJ), so I am not just a high school dropout. But even if I were offered a full-time position, I, and many young security guards like me, would never take it, not because of lack of ability or education, but simply because it would not be a job that pays as well as being a part-timer.
